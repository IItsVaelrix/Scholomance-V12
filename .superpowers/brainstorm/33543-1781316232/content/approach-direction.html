A — Lattice → SVG Rect Export

Each PixelBrain lattice cell becomes a crisp <rect> in SVG. Characters stay deterministic and pixel-art in style but scale infinitely — no blur at large sizes. The Actor Forge Lab previews SVG instead of PNG. AI enhancement = Claude reads the spec and suggests palette/detail tweaks fed back into the foundry.

Pros

  • Stays true to pixel-art identity
  • Infinite scaling (great for iso arena)
  • No new art system needed

Cons

  • SVG files are large (one rect per pixel)
  • Same visual style as today — just crisper

  • Closes the Forge → Combat wiring gap

Cons

  • More complex to make feel unique per-spec

C — Hybrid: Pixel Lattice + Wand Glow Layer

The forged lattice sprite is the canonical body (crisp pixel art). A Wand SVG layer sits on top — school-coloured glow rings, eye shine, aura particles — driven entirely by the spec. Two composited layers in the Phaser scene. AI enhancement = Claude proposes an "enhancement descriptor" that the NLP morph engine applies to shift hues + add effect layers.

Pros

  • Pixel art identity preserved + enhanced
  • Both systems earn their keep
  • AI enhancement has a clear, bounded scope

Cons

  • Two rendering layers to keep in sync
  • Most complex to wire into combat
