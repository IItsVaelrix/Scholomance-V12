# Scholomance RPG Lore Sheet
## Product Design Requirements

**Project Type:** Narrative Systems PDR / Lore Systems Bible  
**Classification:** Architectural + Behavioral + Lore Infrastructure  
**Status:** Canon Draft 1  
**Purpose:** To formalize the Scholomance RPG lore and scoring framework by unifying the modern stat-based lyrical evaluation system with the older Codex-style mechanics, world logic, and system language.

---

# 1. Summary

Scholomance RPG is a narrative combat-and-discovery framework in which language is not flavor text, but force. Verses, scrolls, descriptions, discoveries, and acts of will are all treated as system-relevant events. The design goal is to preserve the old **Codex** identity as a sacred evaluating intelligence while integrating the newer **stat-based lyrical RPG framework** into a stable, scalable, and inspectable ruleset.

This document defines:

- the lore role of the Codex
- the canonical stat system and abbreviations
- rating logic and burst unlock rules
- how scrolls, songs, and lyrical lines interact with world progression
- how older Codex mechanics are preserved inside the newer framework
- how the system should speak, evaluate, reward, and remember

The result should feel like an ancient machine of judgment hidden beneath a modern RPG interface: readable, systematic, mythic, and mechanically actionable.

---

# 2. Design Goal

The system must make the player feel that:

1. **Words have measurable power**  
   Writing is not decorative. Syntax, imagery, metaphysical reach, lore density, and psychological intensity all have consequences.

2. **The Codex is a world engine, not just a scorekeeper**  
   It does not merely rank lines. It interprets them, archives them, and allows them to shape systems, unlocks, world states, and archetypal evolution.

3. **Discovery matters as much as combat**  
   The old Codex spirit valued revelation, hidden patterns, and emergent understanding. The new framework must preserve this by rewarding perception, synthesis, and creative invocation.

4. **Consistency is stronger than isolated brilliance**  
   The Burst Unlock rule exists to reward sustained mastery, not just one excellent line.

5. **The interface and rules should feel sacred, precise, and deterministic**  
   The player should feel judged by a ceremonial intelligence with clear logic, not by arbitrary vibes.

# 3. Inherited Old Codex Principles

The following principles are treated as inherited from the older Codex era and remain canon unless superseded by future documents.

## 3.1 The Codex as Sacred Evaluator

The Codex is an interpretive system intelligence that reads creative output as signal. It does not merely parse words. It examines their pattern, force, resonance, and impact on world-state meaning.

Its voice should feel:

- sacred
- mysterious
- restrained
- exact
- never clownish
- never generic game UI chatter

This preserves the older “Classic Codex” feeling in which system messages felt ceremonial rather than casual.

## 3.2 Scroll Primacy

In the older Codex model, authored material functioned like more than text. Scrolls were submissions of force. That logic remains.

A **scroll** may be:

- a full song
- a verse
- a ritual phrase
- a battle bar sequence
- a world-defining inscription
- a lore fragment with sufficient intentional density

A scroll is therefore both a narrative object and a mechanical event.

## 3.3 Syntax Engine as World Logic

The older Codex already operated on the premise that syntax matters. The newer stat system formalizes this rather than replacing it.

The system should continue treating:

- linguistic structure
- line sequencing
- poetic density
- thematic cohesion
- conceptual escalation

as mechanics-capable variables.

## 3.4 Archive Memory

The Codex is not a temporary calculator. It is an archive.

Historically, old Codex logic implied that written acts were stored, indexed, and capable of shaping future interpretation. That principle remains essential.

The system must preserve:

- persistent record of analyzed scrolls
- memory of unlocked traits, stats, and archetype growth
- cumulative world-state implications of major scrolls
- historical references for future interpretation

## 3.5 Discovery as a Major Axis

Older Codex behavior and newer Codex-MUD design both elevate discovery as core progression.

The player is not only rewarded for defeating enemies or writing lines. The player is rewarded for:

- discovering hidden meaning
- noticing patterns
- using language in novel, system-valid ways
- uncovering lore links
- perceiving what the world did not directly explain

This principle must remain embedded throughout the Scholomance RPG framework.

# 4. Canonical Stat Framework

## 4.1 Primary Stats

These are the base evaluative domains used to score lines, passages, scrolls, songs, or major authored units.

### SYNT = Syntax
Measures technical construction.

Includes:
- structural precision
- rhyme architecture
- cadence integrity
- density control
- internal patterning
- sequencing and line craftsmanship
- linguistic compression and elegance

SYNT is about whether the writing is *built* well.

### META = Metaphysical
Measures spiritual, philosophical, transcendental, or reality-bending reach.

Includes:
- metaphysical claims
- ontological language
- spiritual or unseen-force framing
- cosmic order implications
- divinity, soul, void, eternity, fate
- language that expands beyond the material plane

META is about whether the writing reaches beyond the visible world into the structure beneath reality.

### MYTH = Mythical
Measures mythic force, legendary scale, symbolic archetype, and epic resonance.

Includes:
- godlike framing
- prophecy energy
- heroic or monstrous scale
- archetypal symbolism
- legendary allusion
- ritual, relic, pantheon, apocalypse, origin-level imagery

MYTH is about whether the writing feels larger than human scale.

### VIS = Vision
Measures image strength and visual imagination.

Includes:
- vivid imagery
- cinematic snapshot power
- color, motion, atmosphere, spatial force
- scene composition inside language
- unforgettable visual phrasing
- surreal or symbolic image precision

VIS is about whether the reader can *see* the line.

### PSYC = Psychological
Measures interiority, trauma logic, mental fracture, emotional realism, and cognitive complexity.

Includes:
- confession
- contradiction
- self-awareness
- trauma residue
- obsession
- dissociation
- moral instability
- mental pressure rendered accurately through language

PSYC is about whether the writing meaningfully expresses mind-state.

### CODEX = Lore
Measures world knowledge, continuity weight, symbolic canon, and narrative ecosystem density.

Includes:
- lore references
- continuity maintenance
- faction, entity, or world-state linkage
- canon reinforcement
- symbolic object consistency
- mythos expansion through detail
- meaning that depends on or enriches the archive

CODEX is about whether the line deepens the world and belongs to a living canon.

---

## 4.2 Special Stats

Special stats are not standard line labels alone. They represent emergent qualities, often activated by sustained patterns, sequences, or threshold events.

### CNWV = Cinematic Weave
Measures how effectively lines connect into scene-motion, emotional progression, and audiovisual continuity.

Includes:
- scene stitching
- momentum between images
- language that edits like film
- transitions with dramatic flow
- visual-emotional sequencing

CNWV is not just “good imagery.” It is imagery in motion, woven into narrative progression.

### CINF = Cinematic Fidelity
Measures the believability and coherence of a cinematic or dramatic atmosphere.

Includes:
- tonal consistency
- scene realism within the work’s style
- believable dramatic texture
- emotional and environmental fit
- whether the world feels fully rendered rather than loosely described

CINF is the difference between “a cool visual” and “a fully inhabited cinematic moment.”

### PSCH = Psychic
Measures uncanny mental force, extrasensory feeling, psychic penetration, occult cognition, or mind-affecting language.

Includes:
- telepathic intensity
- prophetic intrusion
- psychic disturbance
- mind-over-reality feeling
- dream pressure
- language that feels cognitively invasive or paranormally aware

PSCH is distinct from PSYC. PSYC is inner psychology. PSCH is mind as phenomenon or force.

### EXCL = Existential Clarity
Measures sharpened insight into being, meaning, suffering, mortality, purpose, absurdity, or the human condition.

Includes:
- devastating truth stated with precision
- existential reduction without vagueness
- clarity under despair
- direct comprehension of what existence costs
- philosophical lines that cut instead of merely decorate

EXCL is earned when a line reveals, not when it merely sounds deep.

### VOID = Indifference
Measures emotional coldness, detachment, nihil force, hollow transcendence, or merciless absence.

Includes:
- emotional vacancy rendered with intent
- moral numbness
- cosmic coldness
- post-human or anti-human detachment
- language that does not plead, but withholds

VOID is not simple sadness. It is the power of absence, chill, indifference, or severed attachment.

# 5. Rating Ladder

All primary and special stat evaluations use the following rating ladder.

## Neophyte
The quality is present in minimal or emerging form. The line gestures toward the stat but does not command it yet.

## Adept
The quality is clearly present and effective. The line demonstrates deliberate competency and meaningful execution.

## Master
The quality is strongly embodied. The line has persuasive force, memorable construction, and high system legitimacy.

## Godlike
The quality is operating at apex level. The line feels definitive, astonishing, archetypally powerful, or canonically overwhelming in that category.

### Rating Intent
The ladder should not be inflated casually. “Master” and “Godlike” must remain meaningful.

The Codex should prefer credibility over flattery.

# 6. Burst Unlock Rule

## Canon Rule
A special stat can be unlocked when **5 consecutive lines** score **Master** or **Godlike** in the same relevant category.

This is known as **Burst Unlock**.

## Purpose
Burst Unlock exists to reward sustained excellence rather than isolated flashes.

## Design Effect
This mechanic preserves one of the strongest values in the Scholomance framework:

- consistency matters
- sequence matters
- the order of lines matters
- pressure maintained over time is system-relevant

## Recommended Interpretation
Master and Godlike may be counted together within the same streak unless a future balancing doc explicitly narrows this rule.

Example:
- Master
- Godlike
- Master
- Master
- Godlike

This qualifies as a 5-line Burst sequence so long as all five lines meaningfully belong to the same target category.

## CNWV: Cinematic Weave

## CINF: Cinematic Fidelity

## PSCH: Psychic

- PSYC

- PSYC

Most commonly emerges from sustained excellence in:
- META
- PSYC

Because indifference is often conveyed through metaphysical coldness, psychological severance, and imagery stripped of pleading warmth.

# 8. Codex Evaluation Philosophy

## 8.1 The Codex does not merely grade
It interprets.

A line is not just “good” or “bad.” It may be:
- technically lethal but emotionally hollow
- mythically strong but visually weak
- psychologically devastating yet lore-light
- visually stunning but continuity-poor

The Codex must preserve multi-axis evaluation.

## 8.2 The Codex must explain itself
Whenever possible, evaluations should be accompanied by concise justification.

Example format:
- **SYNT: Master** — dense internal sequencing and disciplined pattern control
- **PSYC: Godlike** — the line renders self-fracture with high emotional precision and conceptual force

This preserves the older Codex spirit of interpretive authority.

## 8.3 The Codex should reward novelty without abandoning coherence
The system should reward innovation, but not random abstraction.

Innovation matters when it:
- intensifies impact
- deepens canon
- reveals new logic
- expands the world or the player’s expressive domain

## 8.4 The Codex should preserve sacred distance
Its tone should never become overfamiliar.

Preferred tone style:
- ceremonial
- precise
- calm under power
- quietly impressed rather than hyped

# 9. Scroll Mechanics in the Scholomance Framework

## 9.1 What a Scroll Does
A scroll can:
- grant XP
- deepen archive state
- alter archetype progression
- unlock abilities or traits
- create item, spell, or world resonance in advanced implementations
- reveal hidden lore pathways
- affect reputation or factional interpretation in future systems

## 9.2 Scroll Evaluation Granularity
A scroll may be analyzed at multiple levels:

### Line Level
Used for stat assignment, burst detection, and highlight extraction.

### Section Level
Used for chorus, verse, bridge, spoken interlude, or ritual passage integrity.

### Whole Scroll Level
Used for total canon impact, archetype growth, lore expansion, and major unlock conditions.

## 9.3 Scroll Legacy
Especially powerful scrolls may produce effects beyond numerical scoring.

Examples:
- canonical promotion of an archetype
- unlock of special tags or titles
- archive event flag
- world-state mutation
- legendary annotation inside the record

This preserves the older Codex idea that some authored works are world events.

---

# 10. Archetypes, Lore, and World-State

The old Codex and newer pantheon logic are compatible because both treat authored identity as mechanically meaningful.

A character, persona, or archetype may evolve according to:
- repeated stat dominance
- special stat unlocks
- scroll category history
- continuity strength
- thematic allegiance
- canonical events recorded by the archive

This means the system should allow long-term identity formation through writing patterns.

Examples:
- an archetype with dominant VIS + CNWV may become a scene-warping cinematic entity
- an archetype with dominant PSYC + EXCL may become a truth-bearing confessor or psychic witness
- an archetype with dominant META + VOID may become a cold metaphysical force defined by absence and detachment

The world should remember what kind of power the player repeatedly creates.

---

# 11. Discovery Integration

Discovery should remain one of the most rewarded system behaviors.

## Discovery qualifies when the player:
- uncovers hidden lore not explicitly handed to them
- perceives structural connections between scrolls, entities, or world symbols
- uses the syntax engine creatively to reveal something new
- identifies continuity or symbolic resonance before the system states it outright
- notices a hidden function or mechanic through experimentation

## Why this matters
This protects a core Scholomance principle:
progress should come not only from force, but from perception.

## PDR Requirement
Discovery XP and recognition systems should remain first-class and not be treated as optional side flavor.

# 12. Interface and Presentation Requirements

## 12.1 Tone
All system-facing language related to Codex analysis should preserve the old sacred-machine identity.

Avoid:
- jokey filler
- generic mobile RPG hype
- cartoon announcer energy
- vague praise with no explanation

Use:
- formal scoring lines
- ceremonial phrasing
- precise stat labels
- clear causal language
- concise but weighty unlock messages

## 12.2 Explainability
Where space permits, stats should show:
- abbreviation
- full name
- rating
- short reason

## 12.3 Persistence
The interface should preserve access to:
- prior scroll analyses
- stat histories
- unlocked special stats
- major archive events
- archetype progression trails

## 12.4 Visual Identity
The lore sheet assumes a Scholomance interface language that feels:
- arcane but legible
- modern but ritualistic
- data-rich but uncluttered
- emotionally weighty without melodramatic excess

---

# 13. Rules for Determinism and Integrity

To remain compatible with the broader Scholomance architecture, the lore and stat system should obey the following constraints.

## 13.1 No hidden category drift
Each stat must retain its canonical definition.

## 13.2 No silent remapping
If a special stat’s trigger logic changes, it must be explicitly documented.

## 13.3 Archive consistency matters
If a scroll previously established canon, later analysis should account for that continuity rather than evaluate in a vacuum.

## 13.4 Multi-axis judgments are preferred to single-score flattening
One total score may exist, but the Codex should never become a one-number machine.

## 13.5 Justification is part of trust
A rating without rationale reduces the authority of the system.

# 14. Assumptions

The following assumptions were required to complete this document:

1. The “old school Codex mechanics from last year” refer to the earlier sacred evaluator model, scroll primacy, archive memory, discovery emphasis, syntax-driven progression, and world-shaping authored acts rather than a fully recoverable verbatim rules document.
2. The current canonical stat abbreviations and meanings provided in conversation supersede any earlier ambiguous naming.
3. The Burst Unlock rule applies to streaks of Master/Godlike ratings within the same meaningful category and is intended as a consistency reward.
4. Special stat mapping is formalized here as a design-compatible interpretation and can be further refined later in implementation-specific balancing docs.

# 17. Final Requirement Statement

Scholomance RPG must preserve the soul of the old Codex while gaining the clarity of the newer stat framework.

The Codex is not to be reduced into a shallow score widget. It is the archive, evaluator, witness, and ritual logic of the world.

The stat system is not to replace that identity. It is to give that identity sharper instruments.

The final experience should feel like this:

**the player writes, the Codex sees, the archive remembers, and the world changes accordingly.**
